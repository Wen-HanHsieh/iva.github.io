IVA: Instruct-Verify-and-Act

Teaching Vision-Language-Action Models to Reject the Impossible

Wen-Han Hsieh*, Elvis Hsieh*, Dantong Niu, Roei Herzig, David M. Chan, Trevor Darrell
UC Berkeley
arXiv Code & Dataset

Abstract

Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models enable robots to follow natural language instructions grounded in visual perception and actions. However, most assume instructions are always feasible. We propose IVA (Instruct-Verify-and-Act), a unified framework that (i) detects when an instruction cannot be executed due to a false premise, (ii) generates clarifications or corrections, and (iii) grounds plausible alternatives in perception and action. IVA is trained with a semi-synthetic dataset of true and false-premise instructions, enabling robust detection and correction. Experiments on RLBench tasks show IVA improves false premise detection accuracy by 58.9% and increases successful responses in false-premise scenarios by 27.9% compared to baselines:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}.

Key Results

  • False Premise Detection: 100% accuracy on easy false premises, 17.8% on hard false premises.
  • Improved Success Rates: +27.9% improvement in false-premise handling compared to LLaRVA baseline:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}.
  • Stable True-Premise Performance: Maintains ~35% task success rate on true-premise tasks, comparable to baseline.
